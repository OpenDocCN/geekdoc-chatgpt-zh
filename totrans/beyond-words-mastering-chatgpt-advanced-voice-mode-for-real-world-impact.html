Chapter 3: Setting up Advanced Voice Mode like a Pro

Let’s get straight into it.

First things first: if you want to set up Advanced Voice Mode like a pro, you’ll need the ChatGPT app.

This, obviously, isn’t negotiable.

This may change in the future, potentially after I write this.

Now, let's start with a common misconception. You can't simply type a prompt, switch to Advanced Voice Mode, and expect ChatGPT to magically pick up where your text left off.

It doesn’t work like that.

Think of it like trying to have a conversation while switching languages mid-sentence. Confusing and not very effective.

Instead, you’re going to need a secret weapon: Custom Instructions.

Where to Find Them

Go to Settings → Personalization → Customizations.
It’s not buried in some obscure submenu, but it’s not glaringly obvious either. This is where the magic happens.

The Two Boxes (or, as I call them, the Brain and the Mouth)

Once you open Customizations, you’ll see two fields staring back at you like blank pages begging to be filled.

These boxes are more than just text fields-they’re the core of your voice setup.

  1. Box 1 is the Brain.
    This is where you input context about the GPT. Who is it? What is it meant to be doing? What role is it? What action? This is the box where you pour in the details of how ChatGPT should think, act, and prioritise its responses. Think of it as a set of marching orders. The more precise you are here, the more human (and useful) your interactions will feel. But do not go overboard. Simple is best here.
     
  2. Box 2 is the Mouth.
    Now, this one is all about delivery. How should ChatGPT sound? Should it speak with enthusiasm like a motivational speaker, or with calm reassurance like a therapist? Should it lean into humour or stick to the facts? Here’s where you can even specify things like speaking pace or inflection. Or even accent and language. Basically, this is where you tell ChatGPT exactly how to articulate every single word.

Fixing Shortcomings of Advanced Voice Mode

Like any tool, Advanced Voice Mode isn’t perfect. It’s got its quirks, but that’s where these hacks come into play.

We’ll address a few common issues and come up with quick solutions to keep your conversations smooth and responsive.

1. Interruptions: Control the Flow of the Conversation

One of the more frustrating aspects of Advanced Voice Mode is the low latency. If you stop talking for around 3 seconds, it assumes you’re done and jumps in with a response.

Not ideal if you’re mid-thought and just pausing to breathe.

The Hack? Add this line to your Brain section:

"Respond to all of my questions, thoughts, and musings with 'Mhm' until you hear the phrase 'I’m done.' Then, give your thoughts and opinions based on your custom instructions."

What does this do? It gives you full control over when ChatGPT speaks.

You can talk for as long as you need without the fear of getting cut off.

ChatGPT will patiently listen, dropping in an occasional “Mhm” to let you know it’s still there.

Once you’re finished, simply say “I’m done,” and it will provide a thoughtful, context-driven response based on your instructions.

This works almost like a walkie-talkie system where you say “over” when you’ve finished your transmission.

Except, in this case, it’s “I’m done,” and you’re free to speak at length.

2. Understanding Emotion: Making Responses Feel Real

One of the easiest ways to break immersion with Advanced Voice Mode is when the AI delivers a response that feels cold, emotionless, or totally out of sync with your mood. Imagine venting your frustrations only to get a robotic, tone-deaf reply. Pretty frustrating.

The Hack? Inject some emotional intelligence by adding this line to the Brain section:

"Listen to my voice and choice of words. Note my emotions and respond accordingly. From time to time, acknowledge my emotions."

This makes ChatGPT a little more human. It will actively listen to how you’re speaking. Your tone, your word choices.

Then adjust its responses to match your emotional state. So, if you’re excited, it’ll amp up its enthusiasm. If you’re upset, it’ll bring in a touch of empathy.

This turns what could have been a cold, transactional interaction into something that feels conversational and responsive to you, not just your words.

3. Stay on Topic: Limiting Unwanted Responses

Sometimes ChatGPT can go off on tangents or throw in information you didn’t ask for.

While that’s fine in text mode, it can be a bit jarring in voice mode where you want things to stay focused.

The Hack? Add this to your Brain settings:

"Answer only the specific question or task I ask. Do not provide additional information unless requested."

This will keep the responses tight and focused. It’s especially useful when you’re in a hurry or dealing with complex tasks that don’t need extra layers of explanation.

You’ll get exactly what you asked for, nothing more, nothing less.

4. Command Flexibility: Dynamically Changing Settings

ChatGPT’s default settings are great, but sometimes you need to adjust them on the fly. Without diving back into Custom Instructions.

The Hack? Teach ChatGPT to respond dynamically by adding this:

"Adapt to my live commands during our conversation. If I ask for a change in tone, pace, or style, adjust immediately without resetting the original custom instructions."

This gives you more real-time control over how ChatGPT behaves.

If it’s speaking too slowly, you can say, “Speed up a bit,” and it will immediately respond without ditching all the other settings.

This ensures that it adapts to your evolving needs mid-conversation without losing its “brain.”

5. Handling Follow-Up Questions: Keep Context Intact

Have you ever asked a follow-up question, only for ChatGPT to lose track of the conversation’s flow?

This can be a problem in voice mode, where the interaction is supposed to feel natural and continuous.

The Hack? Add this prompt to the Brain:

"Keep track of our ongoing conversation and ensure that responses reflect the context of previous questions."

With this in place, ChatGPT will stay aware of the conversational thread and respond to follow-up questions based on prior context.

It won’t “reset” the moment you ask something new, making the experience more fluid and natural.

The Fix:

Pro Tip:
