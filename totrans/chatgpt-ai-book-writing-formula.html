Prompt AI to Get What You Want

When it comes to conversing with ChatGPT, the quality of the prompts or questions used can greatly influence the success of your conversation and results.

First, let’s clarify the difference between a question, a prompt, and prompt engineering (all terms used in this context).

A question is a direct inquiry seeking specific information. It’s typically straightforward and expects a clear, focused answer. Example: “What is the capital of France?

Answer: Paris

A prompt is a broader term that includes questions but also encompasses instructions, requests, or statements designed to elicit a response from an AI. Prompts can be open-ended or specific, and they don’t always take the form of a question. For instance, “Describe the Eiffel Tower” is a prompt but not a question.

Prompt Engineering is the strategic process of crafting and refining prompts to optimize AI responses. It involves carefully constructing the language, structure, and context of the prompt to guide the AI towards producing more accurate, relevant, or specific outputs.

To illustrate:

  • Question: “When was the Eiffel Tower built?”

  • Prompt: “Tell me about the construction of the Eiffel Tower.”

  • Prompt Engineering: “Provide a detailed timeline of the Eiffel Tower’s construction, including key dates, challenges faced, and the primary materials used. Format your response as a bulleted list with no more than 5 main points.”

In this progression, we move from a simple request for information to a more expansive and complex approach.

Mistakes to Avoid in Prompt Engineering

1.      Being Too Vague or Ambiguous

Don’t: Ask broad prompts like “Tell me about productivity.” This is like walking into a library and shouting, “Tell me everything!” You’ll get too much information, and most of it won’t be useful.

Do: Narrow down the question. For example, “Explain how mindfulness meditation can reduce stress and improve focus in a high-pressure work environment.”

2.      Overloading the Prompt with Too Many Questions at Once

Don’t: Ask multiple questions in one prompt, like “Explain the benefits of meditation, how it reduces anxiety, and how to start a meditation practice.” The AI might give brief, shallow answers to cover everything.

Do: Break it down into focused prompts, each tackling one aspect. For example:

“What are the physical, mental, and emotional benefits of regular meditation?” Note the response, then continue…

“How does meditation help reduce anxiety?” Note the response, then ask…

“What are some tips for starting a daily meditation practice?”

3.      Using Open-Ended Prompts Without Clear Direction

Don’t: Use prompts like “Give me ideas on writing a nonfiction book,” without giving clear guidance. This will lead to generic answers.

Do: Provide more direction. For example, “Acting as an experienced book agent for self-help books, give me 10 ideas for an EFT Tapping practitioner to write a bestselling client-attraction book.”

4.      Neglecting to Set a Tone, Style, or Context

Don’t: Forget to tell ChatGPT the tone or style you want, or you may get AI-speak as neutral, formal responses.

Do: Specify the tone or context. For example, “Help me advise my client about her top three topics to include in her EFT Tapping book, in a conversational tone, as if chatting over coffee.”

Just Write

Whatever you call it, I’m giving you permission to let go of perfectionism and just write. This concept is about giving yourself the space to produce something raw, messy, and imperfect—the kind of writing you wouldn’t show anyone yet, but which is essential to getting started.

Henriette Anne Klauser, in Writing on Both Sides of the Brain, takes this idea even further by explaining the neuroscience of why we struggle with first drafts in the first place. She reveals that writing involves using both sides of the brain—the right side, which handles creativity and flow, and the left side, which controls logic and critical thinking.

The problem arises when we try to engage both sides at once; the left brain starts criticizing before the right brain has had a chance to generate ideas. The result? Writer’s block, frustration, and endless procrastination.

Right Brain Writing

Klauser offers a powerful strategy: write with your right brain first. Let that side take charge, and don’t worry about getting it right. Use timed freewriting to let your ideas spill onto the page without editing or judging. The goal is to generate material, even if it’s messy or incomplete. Your left brain, the inner critic, can get its turn later—during the editing phase. But for now, it needs to sit quietly on the sidelines.

Parking the Critic

One exercise I love from Klauser’s book is what she calls “parking the critic”. Picture yourself locking your inner critic away in a soundproof room or firmly telling it to wait outside while you focus on creativity. This visualization works wonders for freeing your mind from the pressure to write perfectly. It gives you the mental freedom to explore ideas without fear of judgment.

Trust the Process

These early drafts are where you begin to discover what you want to say. Start with one word, phrase, or idea, and let it evolve. As you begin, the writing itself will lead you somewhere. Trust that process. The key is separating creation from critique. Let the right brain make the mess, and then invite in the left brain to tidy up later when it comes to editing and proofreading.

Book Blurb

A “book blurb” is a brief promotional description of a book, typically found on the back cover or inside jacket. It is designed to hook potential readers by highlighting key themes, giving a taste of the story or content, and encouraging people to buy or read the book.

Blurbs are usually written by the author, publisher, or marketing team. In some cases, they may include short praise or endorsements from other authors, experts, or notable figures, but the core of a blurb is a concise summary that entices readers.

Book Blurb Components

  2. Tone: The blurb reflects the tone of the book, whether it’s light-hearted, serious, suspenseful, etc.

  3. Hook: A compelling reason for readers to pick up the book (e.g., a question or a hint that intrigues or sparks curiosity).

  4. Endorsements (optional): Positive quotes or praise from well-known figures or other authors.

A great book description is both an art and a science. It requires creativity to capture the essence of your story, and strategic thinking to present it in a way that compels readers to pick up your book.

Three Vital Formatting Rules

  1. Break It Up. Readers will thank you for breaking up big blocks of text. Use headings and subheadings to guide them through your content, and make sure there’s plenty of white space. This makes your book more readable and gives your ideas room to breathe.

  2. Keep It Consistent. Consistency is key. Make sure your fonts, headings, and margins are the same throughout the book. This makes your book feel polished and professional and keeps readers focused on your message, not distracted by random formatting changes.

  3. Check Your Work. Before you hit “publish,” always check your formatting on Amazon’s preview tool. It lets you see how your book will look on different Kindle devices and in print. You don’t want any surprises, like weirdly placed images or funky margins. If you’re publishing a print version, order a proof copy so that you can see the physical book and make sure everything looks as it should.

Steps to Publish Your Book on Amazon

Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) is one of the most popular platforms for self-published authors, offering an easy and accessible way to get your book in front of a global audience.

Here’s a step-by-step guide to walk you through the process:

Set Up Your KDP Account

If you don’t already have an account with Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing, start by visiting the KDP website (kdp.amazon.com) and signing in with your existing Amazon account. If you don’t have an Amazon account, create one for free. Once you’re logged in, you’ll see your KDP dashboard. This is where you’ll be able to upload your book, manage your author details, and monitor sales.

Start a New Book Project

From the KDP dashboard, click on the “Create” button, which will guide you to set up your new book

project. You’ll be asked whether you want to publish an ebook or a paperback. Many authors choose to publish both, but you can start with an ebook first and add the paperback later if you wish.

Enter Your Book Details

The first section you’ll fill out is the Book Details page. This includes:

  • Book Title: Make sure it’s written exactly as it will appear on your book cover.

  • Subtitle (optional): Exactly as on the cover.

  • Author Name: List your name or pen name, as you want it to appear.

  • Book Description: (As covered in Step 7: Polish to Perfection) This is your sales pitch, so make it compelling. It’s what potential readers will see when they check out your book’s listing on Amazon.

  • Keywords and Categories: Choose up to 7 keywords or phrases that will help people find your book. You’ll also need to select up to two categories that describe your book’s genre or subject matter. Picking the right keywords and categories is crucial to reaching your target audience.

Publisher Rocket is the most popular software used by authors (myself included) for this task. Created by Dave Chesson of Kindlepreneur to help you in choosing the right keywords and categories to reach your ideal readers.

Upload Your Manuscript and Cover Manuscript: Upload your properly formatted manuscript. For an ebook, this should be in a .docx or .mobi format, but KDP will convert your file for Kindle. For a paperback, you’ll likely use a PDF version that includes proper margins and layout for print.

Cover: Upload your book cover file. If you’ve created an ebook, make sure your cover image is high resolution (ideally 2560 x 1600 pixels). Later, when it comes to the print or paperback, you’ll need a full cover (front, back, and spine) in PDF format. KDP also provides a Cover Creator tool if you need it, though professional designs tend to be more effective for standing out in the marketplace.

Preview Your Book

KDP offers a Preview feature where you can see exactly how your book will appear to readers.

Carefully review both the ebook and print versions to ensure everything looks perfect—no formatting errors, odd spacing, or misplaced text. This is your chance to catch any issues before your book goes live.

Set Your Pricing and Royalties

Next, you’ll decide how to price your book and which royalty option you want. KDP offers two royalty options: 35% or 70%. The 70% royalty rate is available for ebooks priced between $2.99 and $9.99, while the 35% royalty applies to books outside of that range. For paperbacks, you’ll also need to consider printing costs, which vary based on the book’s length, color options, and other factors. KDP will automatically calculate the minimum price you can set to cover printing costs.

Select Distribution Channels

Amazon allows you to distribute your book beyond its own store through its Expanded Distribution option. This includes making your paperback available to bookstores, libraries, and academic institutions. While this may not be essential for all authors, it can help increase your book’s reach, especially if you are targeting a wider audience.

Publish

Once you’ve completed all the steps, you’re ready to hit “Publish.” Your ebook will typically go live within 24 to 72 hours, while paperbacks may take a bit longer.

Launching, Marketing, Promoting

Let’s apply a recognized marketing tool: the AIDA Marketing Model to your book marketing.

1.      AIDA Model (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

The AIDA model is a classic marketing approach that focuses on guiding a consumer through four stages of decision-making. It’s particularly useful in structuring book marketing campaigns:

  • Attention: Your first goal is to capture your potential readers’ attention. This can be done through striking visuals, a compelling book cover, or headline-grabbing promotions on social media.

  • Interest: Once you have their attention, generate interest by showcasing what makes your book unique. Create engaging content, like trailers, podcasts, or blog posts, that highlight key themes, the book’s benefits, or its emotional impact on readers.

  • Desire: Build the readers’ desire for your book by sharing reviews, testimonials, or endorsements from influencers. Previews of the book, exclusive content, or success stories can make the reader feel like they need to read it.

  • Action: Finally, encourage immediate action by making it easy to buy. This can be through calls-to-action, limited-time offers, or providing multiple purchasing options across platforms like Amazon, bookstores, or direct sales.

Apply AIDA to Emma’s Book:

Gentle Pilates for Seniors — 7 Steps to Strengthen, Stretch, and Stay Active at Any Age

1.      Attention

To capture the attention of seniors and their caregivers, it’s essential to position the book as a must-read for those looking to improve their health, mobility, and overall well-being through gentle exercise.

  • Targeted Ads: Use Facebook and Instagram ads targeted at seniors, caregivers, and wellness enthusiasts. Craft messages like “Stay Active, Stay Strong: Gentle Pilates for Seniors” to appeal to your audience’s need for low-impact, effective exercise.

  • Partnerships with Senior Communities: Partner with retirement communities, senior centers, and wellness blogs that focus on elderly health. Offer sneak peeks or free Pilates demos to engage their interest.

  • Eye-Catching Visuals: Create an appealing cover design and book landing page that clearly speaks to your audience with images of seniors enjoying Pilates and headlines like, “Unlock Greater Flexibility and Mobility in Your Golden Years.”

2.      Interest

Once you have their attention, generate interest by explaining the specific benefits of Pilates for seniors.

  • Free Webinar: Emma can host a free online webinar or class on “How Pilates Can Transform Your Health After 60.” During the session, share simple tips and give a preview of the book’s practical exercises.

  • Personal Story: Emma’s personal journey with Pilates should be a key element in piquing interest. Sharing her passion for helping seniors live healthier lives through Pilates creates an emotional connection and adds credibility to the book.

  • Engaging Blog or Social Media Content: Write blog posts or social media content that breaks down the benefits of Pilates for seniors, focusing on topics like “How Gentle Pilates Can Help Prevent Falls” or “5 Simple Stretches to Improve Flexibility.”

3.      Desire

Build the audience’s desire for the book by showing them the tangible improvements they can experience from following the practices:

  • Testimonials and Success Stories: Share case studies of seniors who have transformed their health through Pilates. These should focus on real-life benefits like improved mobility, reduction in chronic pain, and better balance.

  • Free Downloadable Chapter: Offer a downloadable first chapter of the book to spark interest and give them a taste of what they’ll learn. A chapter focusing on the benefits of Pilates for seniors, with a few easy exercises, will motivate them to buy the full book.

  • Early Bird Pre-Order Bonuses: Create excitement by offering exclusive bonuses for those who pre-order, such as access to a private online Pilates class with Emma or a free guide on “10 Easy Exercises to Improve Balance.”

4.      Action

Finally, prompt your audience to take immediate action and purchase the book.

  • Clear Calls to Action: Throughout your emails, webinars, and social media, include a clear and direct “Buy Now” button with text such as “Start Your Gentle Pilates Journey Today.”

You can do this by including a reminder in follow-up emails and/or at the end of the book, with phrasing such as: “If this book has helped you in your journey, I’d love to hear your feedback on Amazon. Your review will help others discover how gentle Pilates can transform their health.”

By following the AIDA model, Emma can strategically capture attention, build interest, create desire, and drive action, ensuring a successful launch for Gentle Pilates for Seniors.

The Top Marketing Tactics every Author Needs

Your number one tactic for a successful book launch that garners you a “bestseller” ranking on Amazon is to gather reviews, reviews, reviews.

Coming a close second is: “always be building an email list”.

Both tactics are covered below.

Gathering “Verified Reviews” for an Amazon Book Launch:

As an author, positive reviews can significantly boost your book’s visibility and sales on Amazon. However, it’s crucial to obtain these reviews ethically and in compliance with Amazon’s policies.

Reviews not only increase your book’s chances of being featured in Amazon’s algorithms but also help build trust with potential readers.

What are Verified Reviews?

Amazon marks a review as “Verified” when the reviewer has purchased the book through Amazon at a price close to the regular price. These reviews carry more weight in Amazon’s algorithms and are seen as more trustworthy by potential buyers.

4 Ways to Encourage Verified Reviews:

1. Soft Launch: Release your book at $0.99 to a small audience first. Call this the review period. Encourage these early readers to buy the book and leave honest reviews. Tell them you’re aiming for 5-10 4/5-star reviews. Hopefully you will get more.

PRO Tip: Suggest they choose only two or three chapters to read initially and provide a review on the content of those chapters. Take the pressure off them feeling that they don’t have time to read the entire book.

2. Engage Your Mailing List: If you have a newsletter, inform your subscribers about your book launch and early-bird price of $0.99 and kindly request reviews from those who purchase and read it.

3. Use Amazon’s Programs: Enroll in Amazon programs like Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) Select, which can help increase your book’s visibility.

4. Book Promotion Sites: Use legitimate book promotion sites to reach potential readers who might be interested in purchasing and reviewing your book. Some popular options include:

  • BookBub: A large platform that offers both free and paid promotion services

  • Goodreads: A social network for book lovers with various promotional tools for authors Bargain

  • Booksy: Focuses on promoting discounted ebooks to voracious readers

  • Kindle Nation Daily: Offers several options for promoting Kindle books to a large audience

Remember to always check the current terms and conditions of these sites, as their policies and effectiveness can change over time.

Practices Amazon Frowns Upon

Avoid these tactics to prevent your book from being penalized and reviews removed:

  1. Paid Reviews: Never pay for reviews or offer compensation in exchange for reviews.

  2. Review Swapping: Don’t engage in “you review mine, I’ll review yours” arrangements with other authors.

  3. Family and Close Friends Reviews: Amazon explicitly prohibits reviews from immediate family members. They also discourage reviews from close personal friends, as these can be seen as biased.

  4. Incentivized Reviews: Don’t offer free products, discounts, or any other incentives in exchange for reviews.

  5. Review Manipulation: Don’t use services or tactics that artificially inflate your review count or rating.

  6. Multiple Accounts: Don’t create multiple Amazon accounts to leave reviews for your own book.

Momentum for 30 Days

Remember, building a solid base of genuine reviews takes time. You want to keep up the momentum of sales and reviews for at least 30 days. But expect to take longer if you want to reach the magic number of 100 reviews (something I have yet to accomplish with any of my books).

Focus on writing a great book and engaging authentically with your readers. This approach will lead to more sustainable success in the long run.

For the most effective way of gathering reviews in advance of publication, I recommend that you use an Advanced Reader Team (ART) in conjunction with platforms like Pubby, NetGalley, and ReadersFavorite.

What is an Advanced Reader Team (ART)?

An Advanced Reader Team (ART) consists of individuals who receive a free, early copy of your book in exchange for an honest review. These can be your email subscribers, social media followers, or existing connections.

Aim to nurture this group by offering exclusive content, such as behind-the-scenes insights or free downloads, in exchange for timely reviews on Amazon. Make sure to provide clear instructions about when and where to post their reviews, particularly on launch day, to create a buzz around your book.

Leveraging Pubby for Amazon Reviews

Pubby.co is a platform designed to help authors gather verified reviews on Amazon. It operates on a point-based system, where you earn “snaps” by reading and reviewing other authors’ books. These snaps can then be redeemed to request reviews for your own book. Pubby’s system ensures that you review books from the pool of authors on the platform, rather than engaging in a direct swap, which maintains compliance with Amazon’s guidelines.

NetGalley for Professional Reviews

NetGalley is a widely respected platform where authors can submit their books for review by professional reviewers, librarians, and book enthusiasts. While NetGalley is more expensive than other services, it is a powerful tool for generating high-quality, professional reviews that can carry significant weight in your book’s credibility. Books on NetGalley are often reviewed by influencers in the literary world, which can be especially helpful for launching a nonfiction book.

What I like about NetGalley is that you get a list of the reviewers who picked your book, should you want to follow up with them.

ReadersFavorite for Quick, Verified Reviews

ReadersFavorite offers both free and paid options for gathering reviews. This platform is particularly useful for securing verified Amazon reviews quickly. The free

option gives you access to genuine reader reviews, while the paid service ensures a faster turnaround. In addition to Amazon reviews, ReadersFavorite also offers editorial reviews that you can use in your marketing materials.

Use Facebook’s advanced targeting features to focus on individuals who follow similar authors or topics related to your book, and make sure your ad has a clear call to action directing them to download the book and leave a review.
